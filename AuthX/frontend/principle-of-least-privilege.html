Principle of Least Privilege

What is the Principle of Least Privilege?

The Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP) is a fundamental security concept that ensures users, applications, and systems are granted only the minimum level of access required to perform their tasks—nothing more.

Why Least Privilege is Important

  • Limits damage from compromised accounts
  • Reduces attack surface
  • Prevents unauthorized data access
  • Improves system stability and control

How Least Privilege Works

  1. User or process is assigned minimal permissions
  2. Access is restricted to required resources only
  3. Temporary privileges are granted when necessary
  4. Permissions are reviewed and revoked regularly

Security Practices Using Least Privilege

  • Role-based and attribute-based permissions
  • Just-in-time (JIT) access
  • Separation of duties
  • Regular access audits

Real-World Applications

  • Operating systems and user accounts
  • Database and cloud service access
  • Enterprise IT infrastructure
  • Healthcare and financial systems
Note: The Principle of Least Privilege is most effective when combined with RBAC, ABAC, MFA, session validation, and device & location checks. AuthX enforces least privilege to minimize security risks.
