Encryption in Transit

Encryption in Transit ensures that data remains secure while it is being transmitted between systems, networks, and devices. It prevents attackers from intercepting or modifying sensitive information.

How Encryption in Transit Works

  1. Data is encrypted before it leaves the sender.
  2. A secure communication channel is established.
  3. Encrypted data travels across the network.
  4. Only the intended recipient can decrypt the data.

Security Practices

  • Use of HTTPS and TLS protocols
  • Certificate-based authentication
  • Strong encryption algorithms
  • Protection against man-in-the-middle attacks

Real-World Applications

  • Online banking and payment systems
  • Secure web browsing
  • API and server-to-server communication
  • Cloud and IoT data transmission
Note: Encryption in Transit is a critical security layer. AuthX combines this mechanism with Encryption at Rest, MFA, Session Validation, and Device & Location checks to ensure complete protection.
