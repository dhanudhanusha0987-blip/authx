Unknown or Risky Devices

Unknown or Risky Devices refer to login attempts originating from new, unrecognized, or potentially compromised devices. These devices may indicate stolen credentials, malware-infected systems, or unauthorized access attempts.

Common Indicators

  • Login attempts from devices never used before
  • Device fingerprint mismatches
  • Outdated or insecure operating systems
  • Devices associated with suspicious activity

Detection Flow

  1. User attempts to log in from a new or unknown device.
  2. System analyzes device fingerprint and environment.
  3. Risk score is calculated based on device behavior.
  4. Additional verification or access blocking is applied.

Security Practices

  • Device fingerprinting and recognition
  • Mandatory MFA for unknown devices
  • Device trust and approval workflows
  • Alerts for new or risky device logins
Note: Unknown or risky device detection becomes significantly stronger when combined with Location Checks, Human Verification, and Session Validation to prevent unauthorized system access.
